Vision

Research findings historically have taken years, even decades, to be fully reflected in practice. Even when research translates into change, it often takes the form of incremental improvements that leave larger, systemic issues unresolved.

My ultimate vision is for researchers to have clear relevance for today’s public policy decision-making, and for decision-makers to stay fully informed of cutting-edge research.

Mission

My work centers on collaboration with scholars, governments, and communities to bridge knowledge and action.

I engage with stakeholders to integrate current evidence into real-world policy, practice, and service change. This involves coordinating with diverse points of view to identify common priorities, design appropriate methodologies, and plan successful activities for applying policy research.

In addition, I lead the development of content and deliverables to meet knowledge-to-action needs. This involves crafting messages, plain language, and visualizations to summarize and explain research findings and data. I produce translational material for clients, as well as for a policy-focused blog.

In my view, public policy holds a critical role in addressing issues like Indigenous cultural safety, institutional and structural racism, and climate justice. Research has a similar responsibility to emphasize cultural relevance, holistic and wellness-oriented perspectives, and upstream interventions in our shared body of knowledge. I strongly believe policy-focused KT and related approaches are key to meeting these challenges.
